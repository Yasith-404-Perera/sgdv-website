Our Sanctuary

A school where silence speaks

Founded in the spirit of the Buddha's teaching, SGDV is a Dhamma school nurturing children in mindfulness, ethics, and the timeless wisdom of the Theravāda tradition. Here, the lessons of two thousand years meet the curiosity of every young heart.

Dhamma Lessons

Ages 6 – 18 · Every Sunday 9:00 – 10:00 AM

Our Dhamma lessons bring the Buddha's teachings to life through Jātaka tales, parables, and age-appropriate storytelling. Children develop a deep understanding of the Four Noble Truths, the Law of Kamma, and the example set by the great disciples.

Lessons are divided into junior (ages 6–12) and senior (ages 13–18) groups, each guided by experienced teachers who bring warmth and wisdom to every session.

Students preparing for the national Dhamma examination receive additional coaching and resources to succeed with confidence.

🧘

Meditation

All ages · Every Sunday 8:20 – 9:00 AM

We teach two foundational practices: ānāpānasati (mindfulness of breathing) and mettā bhāvanā (loving-kindness meditation). Both are rooted in the Pāli canon and suitable for children from a young age.

Guided by Ven. Dhammika, sessions begin with gentle breath awareness and progress to radiating goodwill to family, community, and all living beings — a practice that carries far beyond the classroom.

Parents are warmly invited to join the adult session held concurrently in the main hall.

🎵

Pāli Chanting

All ages · Sundays 8:00 – 8:20 AM & 11:00 – 11:30 AM

Pāli is the sacred language of the Theravāda canon — the very tongue in which the Buddha's words were preserved. Students learn traditional suttas, protective chants (pirith), and the meaningful recitation of the Triple Gem.

We begin with short, melodic chants like the Tisarana and Pañcasīla, progressing to longer suttas such as the Mangala Sutta and Karaṇīya Metta Sutta as confidence grows.

🪔

Cultural Events

All families · Throughout the year

Our cultural programme celebrates the richness of Sri Lankan Buddhist heritage. Students participate in Vesak lantern making, Poson Perahera processions, and Esala observances — connecting devotion with creativity and community.

These events bring together students, families, and the wider community. Children perform Dhamma dramas, recite poetry, and display artwork inspired by the Jātaka tales and the life of the Buddha.

Ven. Daranagama Gnanasiri Thero

Principal · Senior Monk

Venerable Daranagama Gnanasiri Thero has dedicated forty years to monastic life and the education of the young. Ordained at the age of nineteen, he studied at Kelaniya University and has taught Dhamma to thousands of children across Sri Lanka.

His teaching style combines deep scriptural knowledge with a gift for storytelling that keeps even the youngest students enraptured. He oversees the SGDV curriculum and guides the advanced senior students personally.

Ven. Wathumulle Gnanissara Thero

Principal · Resident Monk

Venerable Wathumulle Gnanissara Thero trained at a forest monastery in the hills of Sri Lanka, where he deepened his practice of ānāpānasati and vipassanā over twelve years before joining SGDV as our resident meditation guide.

He leads all Sunday meditation sessions and offers individual guidance to students who wish to deepen their practice. He also facilitates a monthly parents' meditation sitting.
